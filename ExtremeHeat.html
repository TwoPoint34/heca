Reduce Risk from Extreme Heat

Michigan summers are getting hotter and power outages from thunderstorms could prove deadly for vulnerable populations.

As extreme summer heat events increase (https://glisa.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/Climate-Hazards-Great_Lakes_GLISA_October_2023.pdf), so do the health risks of heat exposure, especially during power outages and among vulnerable populations such as the low-income elderly. Action: Pinpoint locations for tree and shrub planting to increase paved surface shading Action: Increase overall biomass to produce a general local cooling effect and contribute to climate change mitigation through increased above and below-ground carbon storage Action: Address specific outdoor comfort issues through shading and precision management of undesired radiant heating Metrics of Success: -Track total number of trees and shrubs planted -Estimate overall biomass increase due to HEcA services -Perform qualitative (can be informal) survey on client outdoor comfort
