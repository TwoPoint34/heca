Reduce Risk from Extreme Heat

High heat and power outages from storms are a dangerous combination for vulnerable populations.

Trees and shrubs reduce summer heat and improve outdoor comfort through: shading of heat-storing surfaces, precision management of undesired radiant heating, and creation of a generally cooler micro-climate (cite). HEcA experts use spatial analysis along with field-based observations and homeowner input to optimize these cooling effects through tree and shrub planting. Woody vegetation also help mitigate climate change through increased above and below-ground carbon storage. When appropriate planting locations and species are chosen, trees and shrubs also reduce stormwater runoff, support native biodiversity, and more.

HEcA's success in extreme heat mitigation can be estimated by tracking the total number of trees and shrubs planted and through estimating overall biomass increase. We can also conduct surveys of client outdoor comfort before and after our interventions. A particularly interested client might even choose to measure average summer temperatures of heat-prone location and compare the results before and after tree/shrub planting.
