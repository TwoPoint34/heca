Support Native Biodiversity

.

Of course, clients can also certainly choose plant species that only offer non-human food. Action: Plant biodiverse GI or encourage it through reduced mowing. Biodiverse GI often is more resilient and better reduces extreme heat and runoff than less diverse plantings Action: Design GI to reduce and filter polluted runoff (incl. salt runoff during winter) to improve downstream water quality and thereby conserve biodiversity Action: Even on already forested plots, adding a shrub understory increases ecological niches under existing trees Action: Use GIS to advance wildlife corridors and improve habitat connectivity Action: Conduct plant and animal inventories and visualize them using GIS tools

Metrics of Success: -Monitor long-term health of GI installations -Assess HEcA contributions to improved wildlife habitat connectivity -(Optional) Client can qualitatively observe and possibly even quantitatively measure changes in number and abundance of species on their property
