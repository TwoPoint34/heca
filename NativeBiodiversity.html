Support Native Biodiversity

Native plants overwhelmingly support native wildlife (cite).


Homeowners can choose whatever plants they want for their green infrastructure, but HEcA specialists themselves only plant species that provide tangible value to humans, native wildlife, or both. In addition, HEcA promotes diverse natural communities through new plantings or simply through reduced mowing frequency. This multi-species green infrastructure is often more resilient over time and more effective at reducing stormwater runoff and extreme heat. Even on already forested plots, adding a shrub understory increases ecological niches under existing trees. Through spatial analysis, we can also integrate green infrastructure on a given property into a broader biodiversity context. For example, optimizing GSI to reduce and filter polluted runoff from roofs or paved surfaces can improve downstream water quality and therefore wildlife habitat. A given property can also be assessed in relation to existing wildlife corridors in order to improve connectivity between habitat fragments.

Homeowners who feel inspired to act as a "citizen scientists" can monitor their property's biodiversity over time through plant and animal inventories. Clients who share data and photos with HEcA can help create a larger picture of impacts as well as let us know how healthy their GSI installations are over time. We can use any data we collect to analyze and visualize gains in biodiversity and wildlife habitat connectivity.
