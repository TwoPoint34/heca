Control Disease-carrying Pests at Home

Promote the best of nature on your property and reduce the worst of it.


To survive and reproduce, ticks need moist, sheltered environments and animal hosts. Mosquitoes require standing water to breed. Neither ticks nor mosquitoes are much for travelling, so homeowners often unknowingly breed their own disease vectors in their own yards. NBHS experts look for pre-existing mosquito, tick, and rodent habitat and develop intervention strategies with clients. We also design our green infrastructure to optimize habitat for desirable biodiversity while making it less hospitable to pests. For example, many people don't realize that long grass is not good habitat for deer ticks (Lyme Disease host), and it does not breed mosquitoes(cite). In addition to habitat design, we can recommend effective, ecological, and economical pest control techniques (e.g. selective traps) and in some cases refer clients to licensed specialists for more aggressive interventions.

Metrics of Success:
• Baseline tick and mosquito counts can be established and monitored over time
• Clients will quickly observe a reduction of disease-carrying pests on their property
