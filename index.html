Nature-based Home Solutions (NBHS)

In recent years, there has been exciting momentum in ecological landscaping, green infrastructure, and climate adaptation. NBHS is a revenue-generating not-for-profit with a mission to plan and implement residential nature-based solutions for stormwater management, pest management, food production, and more. Our approach unlocks the potential of geographic information systems (GIS), low maintenance green infrastructure, cost-minimizing efficiency, and ergonomic labor practices. Inspired by the well-established "home energy audit" profession, our "home ecology audit" offers an ideal starting place and "one-stop shop" for research-based, multi-benefit ecological solutions that:

• Directly address well-defined, measurable environmental challenges
• Maximize positive social and environmental impacts through reducing cost, labor, fuel, and long-term maintenance requirements
• Save clients money and improve their quality of life
• Integrate seamlessly with conventional landscaping services (mowing, snow management, etc.)

Value Proposition:

Save Clients Money and Improve Quality of Life

Our "home ecology audits" distill a disorienting variety of ecological landscaping practices and buzzwords into research-based actions that liberate a property's natural beauty. These actions also offer health benefits (clean air and water, stress reduction, tick and mosquito control), and more than pay for themselves through:

• Reduction of costly basement flooding and foundation damage • Establishment of trees and shrubs that produce food year after year • Reduction of lawn area that requires mowing • Savings on municipal stormwater infrastructure and treatment costs, which are passed on to residents through reduced tax burden and/or stormwater credits## • Savings on utility bills through: shading from trees, water conserving landscapes, rooftop solar power, and/or geothermal heating/cooling • Possible re-direction and/or infiltration of snow melt to reduce icing of driveways and sidewalks

Why: NBHS brings the best of nature to your own backyard while creating skilled, dignified, stable, comfortable livelihoods that do not rely on unpredictable external funding. What: Reduce Localized Flooding (esp. Basements)

Why: Human environments have long struggled to deal with heavy rainfall events, and the frequency and intensity of such events is increasing in Southeast Michigan1. When properly located and designed, green infrastructure (e.g. bioswales, rain gardens2, tree pits) reduces stormwater runoff load on sewers and thereby improves downstream water quality while reducing flood risk (esp. basements). Mindfully-wild green infrastructure can also provide a beautiful, low-maintenance, carbon-capturing addition to any yard.

What: Grow Food

Why: While clients can certainly choose to plant whatever they want in their yards, NBHS has particular passion and expertise in edible plants. Growing your own food optimizes freshness, saves money, improves nutrition, fosters a sense of place, builds community, and is simply a lot of fun. Annual gardens are great, but they're also a lot of work. NBHS can plant a perennial "food forest" that you harvest year after year without re-planting. Choose typical fruit trees (cherries, apples, peaches, etc.) or experiment with native edibles as part of a truly local cuisine inspired by indigenous practices and a spirit of experimentation. Your plants will also likely feed wildlife, which can be viewed as a co-benefit, though the animals will typically leave you ample food after they've had their fill.

What: Control Disease-spreading Pests

Why: As Michigan's climate gets hotter and wetter, we are seeing an increase in diseases and the pests (vectors) that spread them3. NBHS addresses this inherently ecological challenge with ecological solutions that reduce or isolate disease vectors (e.g. ticks, mosquitoes, mice). Without skilled management of one's own yard (i.e. more than merely mowing), many residents unwittingly create ideal habitats for mosquitoes and ticks. Over the decade that we've been lightly managing our yard, Cynthia and I have never seen a tick and have seen a dramatic decrease in mosquitoes.

What: Mitigate Extreme Heat

Why: As extreme summer heat events increase4, so do the health risks of heat exposure, especially during power outages and among more vulnerable populations such as the low-income elderly. Precisely-located trees and shrubs can help reduce these risks and save on summer cooling bills.

What: Support Native Biodiversity

Why: Join a growing community of people working ultra-locally to protect our imperiled natural wealth. Native plants support more biodiversity than non-native plants5 and they better support native wildlife6. Fortunately, many people are starting to consider hummingbirds, butterflies, bees, and other priceless pollinators to be more beautiful than golf course sterility. At our own home, my partner Cynthia and I are practicing the nature-based solutions that NBHS promotes. We initially wondered why passers-by stopped and stared at our yard on summer evenings, but then we realized that they were captivated by the chorus of lightning bugs that have sadly disappeared from so many yards.

What: Other Promising Benefits

Why: NBHS methods are ever-evolving and substantially shaped by feedback from our clients, assuming that our practices remain grounded in sound scientific and economic principles. For example, although Michigan typically suffers lower fire risk than the western U.S., NBHS can incorporate fire-resistence into our landscape design if a client so desires. Also, while green infrastructure may often be less effective in the winter7, it can still reduce runoff and thereby lessen icy sidewalks and driveways. NBHS can also help assess the feasibility of energy and money saving opportunities for a home's exterior, such as geothermal heating/cooling systems and rooftop solar. Ground-source heat pump technology (aka geothermal) is highly-efficient and can run on renewable energy when paired with solar power for summer cooling and wind power for winter heating##. For its part, rooftop solar energy is economical and renewable and when paired with battery storage can provide emergency back-up power during outages.

Scaling-up Positive Impact

Our work on individual properties already offers many benefits to the broader community, including: job creation, improved water quality, lowered disease risk, reduced stormwater management costs, and more. That said, NBHS and its partners can also work directly with local governments and watershed organizations to help them comply with regulations and plan for solutions at a larger scale. One powerful tool offered by our partner organization (AdaptGeo) is a full-jurisdiction interactive web map that offers many valuable green infrastructure insights. The map can be used directly by public planners and they can share their map subscription with members of the community, such as: engineers, architects, real estate developers, landscaping contractors, schools, churches, homeowners/HOAs, and more.
