Home Ecology Advisors

Mission: Use advanced geospatial technology and deep ecological knowledge to precisely place residential green stormwater infrastructure (GSI) that: reduces street and basement flooding, lowers risk from extreme heat, supports native biodiversity, captures carbon, and produces food for people. Through harnessing ecological processes, Home Ecology Advisors (HEcA) saves money, labor, and fuel on GSI installation and maintenance. Our GSI integrates seamlessly into a property owner's existing landscaping practices.

  • About HEcA

Create Nature-based Livelihoods



HEcA brings the best of nature to your own backyard while creating dignified, secure, comfortable livelihoods that do not rely on unpredictable external funding. HEcA's research-backed and field-proven techniques radically minimize costs and carbon emissions by nearly eliminating reliance on large vehicles, heavy equipment, and fossil fuel usage. Our deep understanding of ecological processes enables these cost and energy benefits while using efficient, ergonomic, and health-promoting labor practices.

  • Actions

Reduce Localized Flooding (esp. Basements)

Human environments have long struggled to deal with heavy rainfall events, and the frequency and intensity of such events is increasing in much of the country, including Michigan (https://www.epa.gov/climatechange-science/extreme-precipitation). Beautiful, lightly-managed green infrastructure such as bioswales, rain gardens, and tree pits can reduce stormwater runoff and thereby reduce sewer overflows and localized (e.g. basement) flood risk.

  • Actions

Mitigate Extreme Heat

As extreme summer heat events increase (https://glisa.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/Climate-Hazards-Great_Lakes_GLISA_October_2023.pdf), so do the health risks of heat exposure, especially during power outages and among vulnerable populations such as the low-income elderly.

Grow Food

You'll never find fresher food than what you grow yourself. If an annual garden is not your thing, let HEcA plant perennials so you can harvest year after year without re-planting. Choose typical fruit trees (cherries, apples, peaches, etc.) or experiment with native edibles as part of a truly local cuisine inspired by indigenous practices and a spirit of experimentation. Native plants feed native wildlife, but don't worry, the animals will typically leave you plenty of food after they've had their fill. Of course, people can also certainly choose plant species that only offer non-human food.

Support Native Biodiversity (But Only the Good Kind)

Join a growing community of people working ultra-locally to protect our imperiled natural wealth. I personally find songbirds, butterflies, bees, etc. to be more beautiful than golf courses, and I don't think I'm the only one. At first, I wondered why people stopped and stared at our yard on summer evenings. Then I realized that they were captivated by the hundreds (thousands?) of lightning bugs that call our property home. At the same time, we have never seen a tick in our yard in the decade we've lived here (knock on wood). We've also had a major decrease in mosquitos as we've implemented ecological management practices on our property.

Other Promising Benefits

Our methods are ever-evolving and substantially shaped by feedback from our clients. Although Michigan typically suffers from less fire risk than out west, HEcA is fully prepared to incorporate fire-resistence into our GSI design if a client so desires. An exciting area of exploration for us is the potential for precision GSI to reduce icy sidewalks and driveways. While GSI may be less effective in the winter, the research suggests that it can still offer many significant benefits.
