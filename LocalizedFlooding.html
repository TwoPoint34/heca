Reduce Localized Flooding

Use advanced spatial analysis to precisely locate green infrastructure for optimal runoff reduction.


GIS and remote sensing can be used to identify where rainfall is most likely to flow or pool on a property. In most cases these dynamics are not visible to the naked eye. Depending on the clients' vision for their property, green infrastructure in these locations may take the form of planting shrubs or trees or simply reducing frequency of mowing. This increase in perennial vegetation will slow and reduce stormwater runoff through increased infiltration, interception, evaporation, and surface roughness. Trees and shrubs can also be planted to overhang paved surfaces and thereby reduce rainfall reaching the ground and cooling those surfaces during hot weather. In the case of basement flooding, our GIS analysis can often be used to identify direct or indirect causes of flooding and propose interventions to address them. NBHS personally installs low-maintenance GSI, especially shrubs and trees, though if a client prefers more management-heavy plants (e.g. native wildflowers) we offer referrals to our partner companies.

Metrics of Success:
• Reduced frequency and severity of basement and street flooding
• Field tools (e.g. water level data loggers) can quantify runoff reduction, though they are typically not cost-effective at the residential scale.
