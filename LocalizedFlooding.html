Reduce Localized Flooding

Use advanced spatial analysis to precisely locate green infrastructure for optimal runoff reduction.

GIS and remote sensing can be used to identify where rainfall is most likely to flow or pool on a property. In most cases these dynamics not visible to the naked eye. Depending on the clients' vision for their property, green infrastructure in these locations may take the form of planting shrubs or trees or simply reducing mowing frequency. This increase in perennial vegetation will slow and reduce stormwater runoff through increased infiltration, interception, evaporation, and surface roughness. Trees and shrubs can also be planted to overhang paved surfaces and thereby reduce rainfall reaching the ground and cooling those surfaces during hot weather. In the case of basement flooding, HEcA's GIS analysis can often be used to identify the root causes of flooding and propose interventions to reduce or eliminate it. HEcA installs low-maintenance GSI, especially shrubs and trees, though if a client prefers a more management-heavy design (e.g. coneflower, milkweed) we can offer referrals to our partner companies.

Metrics of Success: -Homeowner or tenant experiences less frequent and less severe localized flooding (especially basement) -(Optional) If clients want to really “geek out” on their GI’s runoff reduction performance, they can use rain gauges and water level dataloggers to estimate infiltration. This field measurement is the only reliable way to quantify runoff reduction benefits, though most clients will be satisfied knowing that GI has a strong research basis for its effectiveness (https://www.epa.gov/system/files/documents/2021-11/bmp-bioretention-rain-gardens.pdf, “Rain garden hydrological performance – Responses to real rainfall events”).
