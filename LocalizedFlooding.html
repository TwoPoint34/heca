Reduce Localized Flooding

Use advanced spatial analysis to precisely locate green infrastructure for optimal runoff reduction.

Properly located and designed green infrastructure such as bioswales, rain gardens, and tree pits can substantially reduce stormwater runoff and thereby reduce load on storm sewers and corresponding flood risk (esp. basements)... beautiful, lightly-managed Action: Use GIS and remote sensing to identify where rainfall is most likely to flow or pool on a property (dynamics not typically visible to the naked eye). Action: Reduce mowing frequency and/or plant deep-rooted perennials in runoff channels and pools to slow and reduce stormwater runoff through infiltration, interception, evaporation, and surface roughness. Action: Analyze aerial imagery to trace past flooding events back to their root cause then address that cause with GI installation Action: Plant shrubs or trees to catch rain over paved surfaces before it becomes runoff Metric of Success: -Homeowner or tenant experiences less frequent and less severe localized flooding (especially basement) -(Optional) If clients want to really “geek out” on their GI’s runoff reduction performance, they can use rain gauges and water level dataloggers to estimate infiltration. This field measurement is the only reliable way to quantify runoff reduction benefits, though most clients will be satisfied knowing that GI has a strong research basis for its effectiveness (https://www.epa.gov/system/files/documents/2021-11/bmp-bioretention-rain-gardens.pdf, “Rain garden hydrological performance – Responses to real rainfall events”).
