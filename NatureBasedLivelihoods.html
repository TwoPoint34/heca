Nature-based Livelihoods

Generate skilled, long-term work that cooperates with nature to solve pressing challenges.


The landscaping profession has long emphasized the domination of nature and creation of essentially sterile lawns. Fortunately, in recent years there has been a growing movement to thoughtfully allow nature back into our yards. NBHS specialists harness deep theoretical and applied ecological knowledge to maximize long-term positive impacts for people and the planet. With an increasingly complex external funding environment, our ability to generate positive impact relies only on the initiative of our clients.

Computers are great and all, but Nature has been efficiently automating processes (e.g. photosynthesis) for billions of years. Working with rather than against natural processes can radically minimize upfront and continuing costs such as heavy equipment, large work vehicles, and fuel needs. For example, using GIS and field-based methods to understand a property's topography can greatly reduce or even eliminate earth-moving (digging). This low-disturbance approach not only saves energy and conserves soil but also substantially reduces the financial barriers required for aspiring professionals to enter this line of work. Deep ecological understanding combined with ergonomic labor practices ensures that our specialists stay healthy, happy, efficient, and actively learning throughout their careers. Critically, be it as a small businesses owner or as an employee, NBHS livelihoods will never be outsourced or replaced by robots.

Metrics of Success:
   • Track total number of NBHS practitioners and monitor barriers to entry
   • Survey NBHS worker satisfaction
   • Monitor practitioner earnings
   • Continuosly identify opportunities to reduce service costs, lower labor requirements, and optimize accessibility of practices
