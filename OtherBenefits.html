Other Promising Benefits

Expand our science-based offerings to better serve people and the planet.



Why: NBHS methods are ever-evolving and substantially shaped by feedback from our clients, assuming that our practices remain grounded in sound scientific and economic principles. For example, although Michigan typically suffers lower fire risk than the western U.S., NBHS can incorporate fire-resistence into our landscape design if a client so desires. Also, while green infrastructure may often be less effective in the winter,9 it can still reduce runoff and thereby lessen icy sidewalks and driveways. NBHS can also help assess the feasibility of energy and money saving opportunities for a home's exterior, such as geothermal heating/cooling systems and rooftop solar. Ground-source heat pump technology (aka geothermal) is highly-efficient and can run on renewable energy when paired with solar power for summer cooling and wind power for winter heating.10 For its part, rooftop solar energy is economical and renewable and when paired with battery storage can provide emergency back-up power during outages. For homeowners pursuing certifications (e.g. LEED), working with NBHS may prove a valuable early step. Fire:   Implement fire-resistant landscaping principles and select appropriate plant species, especially near buildings.

Ice:   Although green stormwater infrastructure is typically most effective in warmer temperatures, carefully-designed installations can reduce snow melt runoff and thereby reduce icy sidewalks and driveways. NBHS can use high-resolution imagery to position green infrastructure downstream of concentrated snow melt and upstream of ice-prone surfaces. We can also analyze soils and runoff flows to determine if depaving or permeable paving are viable options... porous pavement

Geothermal Heating/Cooling:   NBHS can assess whether a property has the space required for a geothermal system and refer interested homeowners to licensed contractors for next steps (incl. long-term financing options).

Rooftop Solar:   "Michigan has vast solar potential with more sunlight than Germany, the world’s leader (in) solar power."24 NBHS can use aerial imagery and GIS to estimate rooftop solar energy potential then refer to trusted installers.

Real Estate Acquisition:   Whether you're looking for farmland or your ultimate urban homestead, NBHS can assess a property's soils, zoning, hydrology, and more before you "bet the farm."

Metrics of Success:
     • Client observes less winter ice in historically problematic locations
     • Fire-resistant landscaping monitored over time to make sure it still poses minimal fire risk to nearby buildings
     • Clients report back to us on their satisfaction and savings from solar panels and/or geothermal
     • Buyers of a property give feedback on the role that NBHS played in helping them find the right property
