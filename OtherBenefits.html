Other Promising Benefits

Expand our science-based offerings to better serve people and the planet.


Fire: Implement fire-resistant landscaping principles and select appropriate plant species, especially near buildings.

Ice: Although GSI is typically most effective in warmer temperatures, carefully-designed installations can reduce snow melt runoff and thereby reduce icy sidewalks and driveways. NBHS can use high-resolution imagery to position green infrastructure downstream of concentrated snow melt and upstream of ice-prone surfaces. We can also analyze soils and runoff flows to determine if depaving or permeable paving are viable options.

Ground-source heat pump technology (aka geothermal) is a very efficient method for home heating and cooling. A geothermal system powered from the grid by wind energy is the most promising renewable winter heating strategy for Michigan(cite). NBHS can assess whether a property has the space required for a geothermal system, and if adequate we can refer interested homeowners to licensed contractors to conduct a more thorough assessment. "Michigan has vast solar potential with more sunlight than Germany, the world’s leader solar power"## NBHS can perform a feasibility analysis to estimate home solar potential and to see if space requirements for geothermal may be met. We can also explore some of the potential financing options that may allow a homeowner to pay over time for the installations through the energy savings they provide. If the preliminary numbers crunch, we can then refer homeowners to trusted contractors for further assessment and installation.
